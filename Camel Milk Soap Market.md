- **Soaps with Additives** are gaining traction due to their enhanced functionality. - **Scented and Unscented Variants** appeal to both sides of the consumer base. While scented versions target those seeking aromatherapeutic experiences, unscented variants cater to users with allergies or fragrance sensitivities, including children and individuals with skin conditions like eczema or psoriasis.

**By Skin Type: Formulated for Every Skin Concern**

Camel milk soap’s natural pH balance and unique composition make it suitable for all skin types:

- **Normal Skin** benefits from the gentle hydration and nourishing properties of camel milk, maintaining skin's equilibrium without causing oiliness or dryness.
- **Dry Skin** users appreciate its high lactic acid content, which gently exfoliates and hydrates, alongside fatty acids that provide deep and lasting moisture.
- **Oily Skin** individuals often struggle with breakouts and sebum imbalance. Camel milk soap offers a non-comedogenic solution, effectively cleansing without stripping the skin or triggering excess oil production.
- **Sensitive Skin** consumers find camel milk soothing due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial nature, making it safe for use in conditions such as rosacea, eczema, and contact dermatitis.
- **Combination Skin** benefits from camel milk soap's ability to hydrate dry patches while regulating oily zones, offering a balanced and non-aggressive approach to skincare.
