` `**Key Technologies Driving Innovation in HLA Typing**

**1. Molecular Assays**\
The majority of clinical and research-grade HLA typing is now performed using molecular assays, which offer higher precision and scalability compared to traditional serological methods.

- **Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR)** remains the most widely used technique due to its speed and reliability in detecting HLA alleles. It serves as the backbone of many clinical diagnostic platforms.
- **Sequence-Specific Oligonucleotide (SSO) Probes** are frequently used for low-to-intermediate resolution typing, especially in donor registries. Their multiplexing capability makes them suitable for medium-throughput labs.
- **Sequence-Specific Primer (SSP) Typing** provides improved resolution and is particularly important for resolving ambiguous typing results. SSP is often used in tandem with NGS to validate or confirm findings.
- **Real-Time PCR (qPCR)** adds a quantitative layer to HLA typing by measuring expression levels, which is crucial in cases where differential HLA expression may influence transplant outcomes or disease risk.
- **Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS)** is rapidly emerging as the gold standard due to its ability to deliver ultra-high-resolution allele typing. It is particularly useful in identifying rare alleles, phasing haplotypes, and enabling applications in personalized immunotherapy.
- **Sanger Sequencing** still plays a role as a confirmatory technology. Although not ideal for high-throughput environments, its accuracy and simplicity make it suitable for legacy systems and targeted confirmation.

**2. Non-Molecular Assays**\
While molecular techniques dominate, non-molecular assays still retain relevance in specific settings.

- **Serological Assays** provide important information on surface antigen expression, especially in settings where molecular labs are unavailable or in low-resource geographies.
- **Mixed Lymphocyte Culture (MLC)** is largely confined to research, used to study T-cell responses and immune compatibility at a functional level.

` `**Application Spectrum**

**1. Transplantation**\
HLA typing is a prerequisite for both solid organ and hematopoietic stem cell transplantation. In kidney, liver, lung, and heart transplants, compatibility in HLA genes directly correlates with graft survival and rejection risk. In hematopoietic stem cell transplantation (HSCT), precise HLA matching significantly reduces graft-versus-host disease (GVHD) and improves long-term outcomes in treating hematologic malignancies such as leukemia and lymphoma.

**2. Disease Diagnosis**\
HLA markers serve as critical diagnostic and prognostic tools:

- **Cancer:** Tumor-associated HLA loss or alteration patterns help predict immune evasion and can guide immunotherapy.
- **Autoimmune Diseases:** Specific HLA types such as HLA-B27 and HLA-DR3/DR4 are linked to diseases like ankylosing spondylitis, Type 1 Diabetes, and celiac disease.
- **Infectious Diseases:** Associations like HLA-B\*57:01 and abacavir hypersensitivity have become key components of pharmacogenetic testing.

**3. Research Applications**\
In immunogenetics and population genomics, HLA typing is central to studying disease susceptibility, vaccine responsiveness, and evolutionary lineage. Studies exploring HLA diversity in ethnic populations offer insights into genetic drift, migration, and pathogen resistance patterns.
