- **Support Devices**: Including guidewires and catheters, these continue to play a crucial role in interventional oncology procedures, with steady growth anticipated.

**By Procedure Type**

- **Ablation Procedures**: Accounted for 48.3% of the market share in 2024, due to the rising adoption of thermal and non-thermal tumor ablation techniques.
- **Embolization Procedures**: Witnessing significant demand, particularly in treating liver, kidney, and lung cancers.
- **Radionuclide Therapy**: Expected to grow at the highest CAGR of 7.8%, driven by increasing applications of radioisotope-based treatments for inoperable tumors.

**By Cancer Type**

- **Liver Cancer**: Represented over 37.6% of the global market in 2024, as interventional oncology techniques are widely used for hepatocellular carcinoma treatment.
- **Lung Cancer**: Projected to grow at the highest CAGR of 7.9%, driven by the rising incidence of non-small cell lung cancer and increasing adoption of image-guided tumor ablation.
- **Prostate and Breast Cancer**: Gaining traction with innovations in focal ablation therapies and minimally invasive targeted treatment approaches.

**By End-User**

- **Hospitals**: Held the largest market share in 2024, contributing over 60.4%, driven by the high volume of interventional oncology procedures performed in oncology centers and tertiary care hospitals.
- **Ambulatory Surgical Centers (ASCs)**: Expected to witness the highest CAGR of 7.6%, fueled by the shift toward cost-effective, outpatient-based interventional oncology treatments.
- **Specialty Clinics**: Experiencing growth due to rising demand for personalized, minimally invasive cancer treatment solutions.
- **Research & Academic Institutions**: Continue to play a critical role in advancing interventional oncology through clinical trials and novel therapeutic research.
