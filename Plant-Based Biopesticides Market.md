**Market Segmentation : Plant-Based Biopesticides Market**

**By Type**

**Bioinsecticides**

Derived from plant sources like **neem (Azadirachta indica)** and **pyrethrum**, bioinsecticides target pests such as **aphids, caterpillars, beetles**, and more. They function by repelling insects, inhibiting feeding, or disrupting their life cycles, offering high efficacy without harming pollinators or beneficial insects.

**Biofungicides**

Formulated from plant compounds such as **garlic extract** and **horsetail**, biofungicides combat **powdery mildew**, **downy mildew**, **root rot**, and other fungal infections. They operate either by suppressing fungal growth or stimulating the plant's natural defense mechanisms.

**Bioherbicides**

These natural alternatives to synthetic herbicides often based on **essential oils like clove, thyme, or vinegar** control weed growth by **desiccation, suppression, or toxicity**, minimizing soil and groundwater contamination.

**Bionematicides**

Targeting **plant-parasitic nematodes**, these are produced from plants like **marigold (Tagetes spp.)** or **castor bean**. They either repel nematodes or disrupt their reproduction cycles, ensuring healthier root systems and increased yields.

**By Form**

**Liquid**

Highly favored for **spray applications**, liquid formulations provide **uniform coverage**, fast action, and easy mixing. They are essential in foliar and seed treatment.

**Powder**

Available in **wettable** and **dry forms**, these are suited for seed coating and soil application. Water-soluble powders offer versatility in handling and application.

**Granules**

These **slow-release formulations** are designed for **long-lasting protection** in soil treatment. Ideal for controlling root-zone pests such as nematodes.

**Emulsifiable Concentrates**

Liquid-based and designed for **easy emulsification in water**, these concentrates offer a **potent and stable solution** for wide-acreage foliar spraying.

**By Application**

**Foliar Spray**

The most common method of application, foliar sprays deliver biopesticides directly onto plant foliage. This enables **contact action on pests** like aphids, mites, and fungi.

**Seed Treatment**

Involves **coating seeds** before sowing to offer protection during germination. This method reduces **pre-emergent soil pests and pathogens**.

**Soil Treatment**

Applied directly to the **root zone**, this method is effective against **soil-borne pathogens** and pests such as **nematodes** and **fungal spores**.

**Post-Harvest Treatment**

Utilized to protect crops in storage and during transit from **mold, rot, and insect infestations**, extending shelf life and reducing post-harvest losses.
